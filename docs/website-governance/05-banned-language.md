# EKAS Website Banned Language List
## Prohibited Words, Phrases, and Patterns

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-04-16
**Authority:** Lead Website Systems Engineer
**Enforcement:** Phase 2+ validation agents + manual review

---

## PURPOSE

This list explicitly prohibits generic SaaS marketing language, transformation hype, and competitor-derived phrasing. EKAS must sound like a **serious manufacturing analytics company**, not a generic technology vendor.

**Violation = Page fails approval gates.**

---

## 1. TRANSFORMATION & HYPE LANGUAGE (BANNED)

### 1.1 Transformation Words

❌ **NEVER USE:**
- Transform / transformation / transformative
- Revolutionize / revolutionary / revolution
- Disrupt / disruptive / disruption
- Paradigm shift
- Game-changing / game-changer
- Next-generation
- Cutting-edge / leading-edge / bleeding-edge
- State-of-the-art
- Groundbreaking
- Breakthrough

**Why banned:** Generic hype language that weakens credibility with manufacturing executives.

**Use instead:**
- Replace (manual process)
- Improve (specific metric)
- Eliminate (specific problem)
- Enable (specific capability)

---

### 1.2 Value & Benefit Buzzwords

❌ **NEVER USE:**
- Unlock (value, insights, potential)
- Drive (results, outcomes, growth)
- Deliver (results, value, outcomes)
- Maximize (efficiency, productivity, output)
- Optimize (without specificity)
- Streamline (processes)
- Enhance (without specificity)
- Leverage (technology, data, AI)
- Harness (power of AI)
- Accelerate (without specificity)

**Why banned:** Abstract benefit language that says nothing concrete.

**Use instead:**
- "Reduce shift handoff time from 35 minutes to 2 minutes"
- "Identify root cause in minutes instead of hours"
- "Provide real-time OEE visibility"
- "Enable traceable calculations for IATF 16949"

---

### 1.3 Empowerment & Agency Language

❌ **NEVER USE:**
- Empower
- Enable [users] to (acceptable: "enable real-time visibility")
- Put you in control
- Take control of your data
- Own your data
- Master your operations

**Why banned:** Consumer marketing language, not B2B manufacturing.

**Exception:** "Enable [capability]" is acceptable when specific:
- ✅ "Enable real-time downtime root cause identification"
- ❌ "Enable you to transform your operations"

---

## 2. VAGUE AI & TECHNOLOGY BUZZWORDS (BANNED)

### 2.1 Generic AI Language

❌ **NEVER USE:**
- AI-powered (anything)
- Powered by AI
- Intelligent (without qualification)
- Smart (dashboard, analytics, insights)
- Advanced AI
- Sophisticated AI
- Machine learning-driven (not EKAS architecture)
- Predictive analytics (not EKAS capability)
- Next-gen AI

**Why banned:** Vague, doesn't explain tool-first architecture, sounds like every other AI vendor.

**Use instead:**
- "Tool-first AI architecture"
- "AI selects queries; SQL computes results"
- "Architecturally prohibited from computation"
- "Manufacturing AI that refuses to guess"

---

### 2.2 Generic Tech Buzzwords

❌ **NEVER USE:**
- Cloud-native (unless specific architectural discussion)
- Scalable / scales / scalability (without specifics)
- Flexible / flexibility (without specifics)
- Robust / robustness (without specifics)
- Enterprise-grade (meaningless qualifier)
- Best-in-class (unprovable claim)
- Industry-leading (unprovable claim)
- World-class (hyperbole)

**Why banned:** Empty qualifiers that provide no information.

**Use instead:**
- "PostgreSQL RLS multi-tenant isolation"
- "Deployable on-premise or cloud"
- "Docker container deployment"
- "56 row-level security policies"

---

## 3. INSIGHT & INTELLIGENCE OVERUSE (RESTRICTED)

### 3.1 "Insights" (Use Sparingly)

❌ **AVOID (Generic Use):**
- "Actionable insights"
- "Unlock insights"
- "Drive insights"
- "Powerful insights"
- "Data-driven insights"

✅ **ACCEPTABLE (Specific Use):**
- "Manufacturing intelligence" (EKAS-specific term)
- "Root cause analysis" (specific, not generic "insights")
- "Downtime event attribution" (specific outcome)

**Rule:** If you can replace "insights" with a specific capability, do so.

---

### 3.2 "Intelligence" (Restricted Context)

✅ **ACCEPTABLE:**
- "Manufacturing intelligence" (EKAS product category)
- "EKAS Intelligence Platform" (product name context)

❌ **AVOID:**
- "Business intelligence" (generic BI)
- "Intelligent system" (vague)
- "Intelligence layer" (use "analytics foundation")

---

## 4. GENERIC SAAS TEMPLATE LANGUAGE (BANNED)

### 4.1 Solution & Platform Generics

❌ **NEVER USE:**
- "The [platform/solution/system] for [industry/use case]"
  - Example: "The platform for modern manufacturing"
- "Built for [audience]"
  - Example: "Built for manufacturing leaders"
- "Designed to [generic benefit]"
  - Example: "Designed to maximize efficiency"
- "All-in-one [category] solution"
- "Complete [category] platform"
- "Comprehensive [capability]"

**Why banned:** SaaS template copy found on thousands of websites.

**Use instead:**
- Lead with specific differentiation: "Manufacturing AI that refuses to guess"
- State concrete capability: "10 production-ready metrics with provenance"

---

### 4.2 Call-to-Action Clichés

❌ **NEVER USE:**
- "Get started today"
- "See the difference"
- "Experience the power"
- "Discover how"
- "Learn why"
- "Find out how"
- "See how [we/it] can help"
- "Start your journey"
- "Take the first step"

**Why banned:** Generic CTA language that provides no value context.

**Use instead:**
- "Request a demo"
- "Request pilot deployment"
- "Explore platform capabilities"

---

## 5. SUPERLATIVES & UNPROVABLE CLAIMS (BANNED)

### 5.1 Absolute Superlatives

❌ **NEVER USE (Unless Provable):**
- Best
- Fastest
- Most (powerful, advanced, comprehensive)
- Leading (solution, platform, provider)
- #1 (anything)
- Top (rated, solution, choice)
- Only (unless literally true and provable)
- First (unless literally true and provable)

**Exception:** "First manufacturing analytics platform where AI is architecturally prohibited from computing results" is acceptable IF this is verifiably true and defensible.

**Safe alternatives:**
- State specific capabilities without comparison
- Use measurable specifics: "10 production-ready metrics"

---

### 5.2 Unquantified Performance Claims

❌ **NEVER USE:**
- "Dramatic improvement"
- "Significant reduction"
- "Substantial increase"
- "Rapid results"
- "Instant visibility"
- "Immediate impact"

**Why banned:** Unquantified claims are empty promises.

**Use instead:**
- "Reduce shift handoff time from 35 minutes to 2 minutes" (specific)
- "Identify root cause in minutes instead of hours" (specific)
- "Real-time OEE visibility" (if true)

---

## 6. COMPETITOR-DERIVED LANGUAGE (BANNED)

### 6.1 Do NOT Copy Competitor Terminology

**NEVER copy or paraphrase from:**
- MachineMetrics: "machine analytics", "condition monitoring"
- Plex: "smart manufacturing", "connected factory"
- Tulip: "frontline operations", "no-code"
- Sight Machine: "digital twin", "prescriptive analytics"
- Epicor: "intelligent ERP", "industry-built"

**Rule:** If a phrase sounds like it came from a competitor website, don't use it.

---

### 6.2 Generic Manufacturing Tech Phrases (Use with Caution)

**Overused industry phrases to avoid:**
- "Connected factory"
- "Smart factory" / "Smart manufacturing"
- "Industry 4.0"
- "Digital transformation"
- "Industrial IoT" / "IIoT"
- "Operational excellence"
- "Continuous improvement" (acceptable only if specific to Lean/Six Sigma context)

**EKAS-specific alternatives:**
- "Manufacturing intelligence"
- "Governed metrics"
- "Tool-first architecture"
- "Provenance on every answer"
- "Controlled refusal system"

---

## 7. FILLER & HEDGE WORDS (REMOVE ALWAYS)

### 7.1 Hedge Words

❌ **DELETE THESE:**
- Basically
- Essentially
- Generally
- Typically
- Usually
- Often
- Potentially
- Possibly
- Probably
- Approximately (use specific number or range)

**Why:** Weakens confidence, adds no value.

---

### 7.2 Intensifiers (Overuse)

❌ **USE SPARINGLY:**
- Very
- Really
- Extremely
- Highly
- Incredibly
- Truly
- Actually
- Definitely
- Absolutely
- Certainly

**Rule:** If removing the intensifier doesn't change meaning, remove it.

---

## 8. IMPLEMENTATION-SPECIFIC VIOLATIONS

### 8.1 Overclaimed Capabilities

❌ **DO NOT CLAIM (Unless Documented):**
- "Pre-built connectors for [vendor list]"
- "Dashboard builder"
- "Scheduled reports"
- "Export to Excel/PDF"
- "Predictive maintenance"
- "Machine learning models"
- "FMEA integration" (use "failure analysis workflows")
- "91 metrics" (say "10 production-ready metrics, 91 in development")

**Rule:** If capability isn't documented in source materials or codebase, don't claim it.

---

### 8.2 Customer & Social Proof Violations

❌ **DO NOT CLAIM (Unless Documented):**
- Specific customer names (unless approved)
- "Trusted by Fortune 500"
- "Hundreds of customers"
- "Industry leader"
- ROI percentages without documented case studies
- Testimonial quotes (unless documented)

**Acceptable:**
- "Production deployment with pilot customers"
- "Mid-market manufacturers"
- "Tier 2/3 suppliers"

---

## 9. TECHNICAL JARGON OVERUSE (BUYER PAGES)

### 9.1 Avoid on Homepage & Hubs

❌ **TOO TECHNICAL FOR HOMEPAGE:**
- "Orchestration framework"
- "Semantic layer"
- "Vector embeddings"
- "LLM" (use "AI")
- "PACT agents" (internal architecture)
- "PostgreSQL internals"
- "Row-level security policies"

**Save for:**
- `/platform/ai-assistant` (technical deep-dive)
- `/security` (IT audience)
- `/platform/data-connections` (integration details)

---

### 9.2 Balance Technical Credibility vs. Readability

✅ **GOOD (Appropriate Technical Detail):**
- "SHA-256 hash provenance"
- "ISO 22400-2:2014 compliance"
- "PostgreSQL RLS multi-tenant isolation"
- "Tool-first architecture"

**Context:** These provide technical credibility without overwhelming buyer.

❌ **TOO MUCH (For Homepage):**
Listing specific policy counts, agent architecture details, database schema specifics.

---

## 10. WRITING PATTERN VIOLATIONS

### 10.1 Avoid These Sentence Patterns

❌ **BANNED PATTERNS:**
- "With [product], you can..."
- "Our [solution] helps you..."
- "We empower you to..."
- "Imagine a world where..."
- "What if you could...?"
- "Tired of [pain point]?"
- "Say goodbye to [problem]"
- "It's time to [action]"

**Why:** Generic SaaS copywriting templates.

**Use instead:** Direct, factual statements about capabilities.

---

### 10.2 Rhetorical Question Overuse

❌ **AVOID RHETORICAL QUESTIONS:**
- "What if you could eliminate downtime?"
- "Ready to transform your operations?"
- "Why settle for spreadsheets?"

**Exception:** One rhetorical question per page maximum, and only if it directly sets up the page's core message.

✅ **BETTER:** State the problem directly.
- "Manual shift handoffs take 35 minutes and risk information loss."

**P0 Violation (Must fix before launch):**
- Unsupported capability claims
- Competitor-copied language
- Transformation hype language

**P1 Violation (Should fix before launch):**
- Generic SaaS template language
- Vague benefit claims
- Filler words

**P2 Violation (Post-launch refinement):**
- Minor intensifier overuse
- Single instance of hedge words

## 12. EXCEPTIONS & CONTEXT

### 12.1 When Banned Words Are Acceptable

**"Intelligence"** → OK in "manufacturing intelligence" (product category)
**"Smart"** → OK in "smart contracts" (technical term, not marketing fluff)
**"Advanced"** → OK in "Advanced Product Quality Planning (APQP)" (industry standard)

**Rule:** Technical terms and industry standards are exempt from ban list.

---

### 12.2 Tone Context by Page Class

**Trust Pages (Security, About):** More formal, technical terms appropriate
**Conversion Pages (Homepage, Hubs):** Business language, minimal jargon
**Capability Pages (Detail):** Balanced—business outcome + technical proof
